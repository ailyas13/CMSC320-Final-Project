1. Introduction

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), about 1 in 6 people gets sick by consuming contaminate food, and these foodborne diseases cause 48 million illnesses and about 3,000 deaths each year in the United States. Here

Food safety is an important aspect to reduce the risk of diseases and deaths that are caused by consuming contaminated foods and beverages.

The Licensure & Regulatory Services Program inspects all the licensed retail food establishments in Montgomery County. Using LRSP's Food Inspection data and Data Science skills, I will present and examine the frequency of the inspections conducted in the Montgomery County's restaurants on a routine basis. This will give an insight of how safe and healthy our food choices are and will be in the future.

#6.Overall Predictions and Insights

According to the predictions and analysis, we have found that some of the major cities of the Montgomery county are fulfilling the requirements for their food products to be inspected, and the minor cities are not being inspected as often as the big cities.

In the Inspection, there were two types of inspections that were conducted on a routine basis. The first type, a comprehensive inspection, which is an inspection that evaluates the sanitation, maintenance, and food service operations for the facility. This inspection includes many of the items found in the monitoring inspection, such as critical temperatures and food handling procedures. The second type, a monitoring inspection, involves checking the food service operation for critical food temperatures, equipment temperatures, and general food handling or cleanliness practices. While monitoring inspections are not as detailed as environmental inspections, they help ensure that the facility is operating safely throughout the year. Which we saw through the Inspection Results from a dataset while representing data.

The frequency of inspections is based on the food-borne illness risks associated with the food being processed at a business place. Based on the risk each facility is designated as high, medium, or low priority. High priority risk facilities prepare food products a day or more in advance or utilize any combination of two or more processes such as a cooking, cooling, reheating and hot holding food over four hours. Moderate priority risk facilities prepare and cook food products which are served immediately or within four hours. A low priority risk facility serve prepackage foods that are not potentially hazardous. Accordingly, Inspections information for each report associated with the Establishment ID, and their results based on high and low frequency are included in the dataset.

This information provides enough of understanding about how and why inspecting food safety is important. Inspection for the food business should be conducted more often to provide a healthy and safe enviroment.
